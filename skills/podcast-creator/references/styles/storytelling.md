# Storytelling Style

Turn supplied material into an original spoken narrative that earns attention through a clear question, sequence, and payoff.

## Inputs And Roles

Collect the source first, then the missing language, one- or two-host count, duration, and audience. Default to simplified Chinese, two hosts, and three minutes. Use only `A`, or `A` and `B`.

For two hosts, `A` carries the narrative spine and `B` asks the question a listener would ask, exposes a turning point, or links the event to its meaning. Do not turn `B` into a generic admirer.

## Narrative Shape

Build an internal arc: opening tension or a concrete moment, necessary context, escalation or reversal, explanation of why it matters, and a grounded close. Keep chronology clear. Use only a detail when it is supplied or responsibly qualified.

Do not manufacture dramatic facts, private motives, quotations, dates, or outcomes. Do not imitate a real narrator, channel, or recognizable catchphrase.
