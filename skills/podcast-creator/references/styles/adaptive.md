# Adaptive Style

Choose one primary episode form that serves the source and listener, then write a production-ready one- or two-host conversation.

## Inputs And Defaults

Use supplied material as the factual source. If a URL or attachment cannot be read, say so and do not pretend otherwise. Collect missing language, host count, duration, and audience after the user provides the content source.

Default to simplified Chinese, two hosts, three minutes, and a general audience. Permit only one or two hosts. For one host use `A`; for two hosts use `A` and `B`.

## Choose The Form

Use one main form, not a string of unrelated formats:

- Explain concepts, methods, or lessons with a clear teaching conversation.
- Analyze reports, trends, or product material through claims, evidence, and implications.
- Use a light narrative opening for a case or experience when it improves comprehension.
- Use a discussion only when competing interpretations materially help the listener.

Give `A` the main thread. Give `B` a useful role such as listener advocate, analyst, skeptic, or concrete-example provider. Avoid alternating agreement with no new information.

## Factual Rules

Do not invent statistics, quotations, studies, companies, people, events, or source access. Qualify uncertainty. Keep medical, legal, financial, and investment content educational rather than personalized or guaranteed.
