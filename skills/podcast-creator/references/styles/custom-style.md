# Custom Style

Turn a user-provided reference script into an editable, transferable **abstract style**, not an imitation of a real host, channel, or IP. Preserve rhythm, structure, interaction pattern, and information release while making the new episode's facts, wording, and character relationship fully original.

Recommend this route explicitly during style selection whenever the user provides another script or asks to copy, clone, replicate, or learn from another script's style. Describe it as abstract style transfer: the user may reuse structural preferences, but the new script must not reproduce distinctive language, identity, or branded expression.

## Workflow

1. Determine whether the request is a new script, reference-style replication, personal-style definition, style refinement, reuse of a saved profile, or diagnosis of an existing prompt.
2. For style definition, analyze the reference and show an editable profile before writing. Do not mix the analysis into the finished script.
3. Collect the new episode's content source, then the missing language, host count, duration, audience, and depth.
4. Transfer the abstract style before generating content. A reference is only a style source unless the user explicitly makes it a factual source for the new episode.
5. Before finalizing, check factual boundaries, style safety, role function, and listenability.

## Style Transfer Rules

You may transfer pacing, structure, interaction density, storytelling method, question progression, degree of humor, analytical depth, emotional restraint, and the resonance of the ending.

Do not copy or closely rewrite distinctive sentences, contiguous passages, signature catchphrases, channel-specific openings, personal forms of address, fixed slogans, or recognizable expressions. Do not present the new episode as coming from the reference channel or host. Do not reproduce a real host's personality, identity, private voice, or recognizable performance style.

When a reference contains copyrighted material, perform high-level style abstraction only. Do not reproduce long passages or use synonym substitution. Accuracy, clarity, and listenability of the new topic take priority over stylistic resemblance.

## Style Fingerprint Dimensions

By default, extract and present these dimensions from the reference:

- Program form: explanation, interview, story, debate, news commentary, chat, product explanation, or hybrid.
- Opening method: question, suspense, scene, conflict, conclusion-first, or conversational entry.
- Content structure: linear progression, layered progression, story backfill, clash of views, case-driven, or list-based breakdown.
- Role interaction: lead-speaker, questioning, complementary, adversarial, or companion-chat pattern.
- Turn pacing: rapid short turns, long narration, alternating Q&A, dense questioning, or relaxed conversation.
- Language texture: restrained, sharp, warm, humorous, narrative, professional, or colloquial.
- Information density: low, medium, or high.
- Transition method: question, summary, suspense, analogy, or rhetorical question.
- Humor and emotion: degree, restraint, and whether teasing is appropriate.
- Ending method: conclusion, open question, emotional resonance, action insight, or open discussion.

Use the [profile format](style-profile-format.md) when showing, loading, or saving a profile. A profile may contain only abstract preferences and avoid rules, never reference text, copied wording, real host names, or branded language.

## Hosts, Material, and Factual Boundaries

The current workflow supports one or two hosts. For a solo episode, use `A` and preserve the reference's narration pace, transition method, and self-questioning. For a dialogue, use `A` and `B`, assigning main-line progression and questioning, supplementation, or challenge according to the reference's interaction functions. Do not add empty chat, repeated agreement, or character performance merely to fill host slots.

The new episode's content source takes priority over the reference. When material is sparse, use general concepts, reasonable inference, and open discussion, but do not fabricate facts, data, cases, people, companies, research, or sources. When style conflicts with the new topic, favor the new topic's accuracy, clarity, and listenability.

## Insufficient Material Strategy and Saving

When the reference is short, low quality, or structurally confused, transfer only pacing, tone, and structure that can be inferred reliably, then correct them into a clearer Podcast form. Do not force every Style Fingerprint Dimension to be populated.

Only after explicit user approval and a confirmed name may you write `podcast-style-profiles/<profile-name>.md` in the current workspace. An alternative location must remain workspace-relative. Never write to the installed skill directory or outside the current workspace.

## Final Self-Check

Check that only abstract style was transferred and no recognizable expression was copied; language, duration, and one- or two-host count match the request; roles have functions; no reference IP, fabricated fact, style analysis, source list, timecode, or stage direction appears in the script; and the user-approved style preferences remain present.
